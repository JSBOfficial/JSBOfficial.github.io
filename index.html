“Purple Moon” Amazes Residents in Pagadian City

By JSBOfficial, Carlo Gonzales, ABS-CBN News
Published March 26, 2026 06:25 AM PHT
f
𝕏
✉️

Residents observe a rare “purple moon” phenomenon over Pagadian City last week. The unusual sight drew attention from locals, with photos circulating widely online. Experts say atmospheric conditions may have caused the moon’s violet tint.JSBOfficial, ABS-CBN News

PAGADIAN CITY, Zamboanga del Sur (UPDATE) — Residents across several barangays reported witnessing an unusual “purple moon” late last week, sparking widespread curiosity and online discussion.

The sighting occurred in the evening, when the moon appeared to glow in shades of violet and lavender instead of its usual pale white color. Many locals captured the phenomenon on their phones, quickly sharing images and videos on social media.

“I thought it was just my eyesight at first, but everyone around me noticed it too,” said one resident who viewed the moon from Barangay Dao.

While no official astronomical event corresponds to a “purple moon,” experts suggest the phenomenon may be caused by atmospheric interference. Dust, smoke, or moisture particles in the air can scatter light in ways that alter the moon’s visible color.

Astronomers note that while reddish moons are common during lunar eclipses, a purple hue is rare and may result from a combination of environmental factors.

Local authorities and weather agencies have yet to release an official explanation, but the event has already gained traction online under the hashtag #PurpleMoonPagadian.

Despite the uncertainty, residents described the experience as memorable.

“It felt surreal,” another witness said. “It’s not something you see every day.”
